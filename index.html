About Me

From Forestry to Programming

Start of career 2010

I started my career as a BSc Forestry student at the University of Aberdeen in 2010, and then progressed to forest manager's role which I kept till late 2019. Throughout this journey I found it fascinating how information technologies are utilised in the field of natural resources management. My early encounters with programming contributed to this fascination, specifically, through automating excel spreadsheets, designing and managing geo-spatial databases, conducting spatial analysis on forestry data, and producing maps to support work programmes and/or forest management plans. I soon realised that virtually everything in this world can be represented as some form of data, and our ability to work with it, ask questions, and find direction is priceless in the quest of making the Spaceship Earth a more pleasant experience for humans and orther species we get to co-exist with.

Future aspirations 2020

I see my future self as a pragmatic programmer, equipped with in-depth knowledge of languages including but not limited to Python, Ruby, Visual Basic, JavaScript, Java, C#, HTML5, CSS3 and PHP. To me, client-side and server-side programming are equally important in modern application development, hence my determination to become a fully-fledged full stack developer.
